Trading View Market Overs

In TradingView, market overs indicate periods when a stock, index, or any asset may be overbought or oversold. These insights are typically derived from indicators such as the Relative Strength Index (RSI), Stochastic Oscillator, or Bollinger Bands. Overbought conditions occur when prices rise sharply and may suggest a potential reversal or pullback, while oversold conditions imply the oppositeâ€”a potential bounce or upward movement. Traders use these insights alongside other tools to confirm trends, identify entry and exit points, and reduce risk in volatile markets. Market overs are not standalone indicators; they work best when combined with trend analysis and volume data.

Volumes in TradingView

Volume is a crucial metric in trading, representing the total number of shares or contracts traded during a specific period. In TradingView, volume bars help traders gauge market participation and the strength of a price move. High volume often signals strong interest and can confirm the validity of price trends or reversals, while low volume might indicate weak participation and uncertain price movements. Advanced tools like the Volume Weighted Average Price (VWAP) and On-Balance Volume (OBV) further enhance the analysis, helping traders identify key levels of support, resistance, and momentum shifts. Accurate volume analysis is essential for understanding market dynamics and making informed decisions.
